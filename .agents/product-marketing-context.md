# Product Marketing Context

*Last updated: 2026-04-15*

## Product Overview
**One-liner:** PawBalance tells dog owners instantly whether a food is safe, risky, or toxic for their specific dog.
**What it does:** A mobile + web app with a 200+ food safety database personalized by breed, age, and weight. Users search any food, browse by category, build home-cooked recipes with AI safety analysis, and scan commercial food labels for hidden dangers. Calculates daily caloric needs per dog.
**Product category:** Pet nutrition / Dog food safety checker
**Product type:** Mobile app (iOS) + Web app
**Business model:** Freemium with 3 tiers — Free (food search + pet profiles), Basic $6.99/mo (AI recipe analysis), Premium $9.99/mo (food label scanner). Annual plans at ~30% discount.

## Target Audience
**Target users:** Dog owners in English-speaking markets (US, UK, Canada, Australia, New Zealand)
**Primary use case:** "Can my dog eat this?" — instant food safety lookup personalized to their dog
**Jobs to be done:**
- Check if a specific food is safe before feeding it to their dog
- Build balanced homemade meals and verify ingredient safety with AI
- Scan commercial pet food labels for hidden toxic ingredients
**Use cases:**
- New puppy owner learning what's safe/toxic
- Home-cooking advocate building balanced dog meals
- Health-conscious owner checking commercial food labels
- Multi-dog household managing different dietary needs (breed/age/weight differences)

## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Dog owners don't know which human foods are safe, which need preparation, and which are toxic — and the answer varies by breed, age, and size.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Google results are generic (not personalized to your dog's breed/weight/age)
- Vet visits for basic food questions are expensive and slow
- Existing pet apps focus on tracking, not safety decisions
- No app combines food lookup + recipe building + label scanning
**What it costs them:** Anxiety every time they want to share food; potential vet bills from accidental toxic feeding; suboptimal nutrition from guessing
**Emotional tension:** Fear of accidentally poisoning their dog. Guilt about not knowing enough about canine nutrition.

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** PetMD food lists, AKC food guides — static articles, not personalized, no recipe analysis
**Secondary:** Generic pet health apps (PetDesk, Pawtrack) — focus on vet appointments and tracking, not food safety
**Indirect:** Googling "can dogs eat X" — inconsistent results, no personalization, no recipe context

## Differentiation
**Key differentiators:**
- Personalized safety ratings by breed, age, weight, and health condition
- AI-powered recipe analysis (not just lookup — analyzes full meals)
- Food label scanner (photograph → ingredient extraction → safety check)
- Bilingual (English + Turkish)
- Privacy-first (no tracking, no ads, no data selling)
**Why customers choose us:** One app replaces Googling, guessing, and worrying. Personalized answers in seconds.

## Objections
| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| "I can just Google it" | Google gives generic answers. PawBalance personalizes to your dog's breed, age, and weight — a food safe for a Labrador might not be safe for a Chihuahua puppy. |
| "My vet handles this" | PawBalance isn't a vet replacement — it's for the 100 daily food decisions between vet visits. Saves time and worry. |
| "Why pay when free tier exists?" | Free tier covers food search. Pay unlocks AI recipe analysis and label scanning — the features that save the most time and prevent the most mistakes. |

**Anti-persona:** People who feed only commercial kibble and never share human food or cook for their dogs.

## Switching Dynamics
**Push:** Anxiety from not knowing what's safe; bad experience from feeding something harmful; overwhelmed by conflicting Google results
**Pull:** Instant personalized answers; peace of mind; AI that checks entire recipes at once
**Habit:** Comfortable Googling (even if results are inconsistent); trust vet for everything
**Anxiety:** "Is the AI actually accurate?" "Will I still need my vet?"

## Customer Language
**How they describe the problem:**
- "Can my dog eat grapes?"
- "I want to cook for my dog but I'm scared I'll use something toxic"
- "Every website says something different"
- "I gave my dog chocolate and panicked"
**How they describe us:**
- "Like a nutritionist in my pocket for my dog"
- "I just search the food and it tells me instantly"
**Words to use:** safe, toxic, personalized, instant, peace of mind, your dog, home-cooked, ingredients
**Words to avoid:** algorithm, data, subscription, paywall, AI (in user-facing — say "smart" or "personalized" instead)

## Brand Voice
**Tone:** Warm, knowledgeable, reassuring — like a trusted friend who happens to be a canine nutritionist
**Style:** Direct, clear, no jargon. Short sentences. Safety-first framing.
**Personality:** Caring, confident, practical, trustworthy
